Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR Code?

A QR code, short for quick-response code, Back in 1994, a man named Masahiro Hara at the Japanese company Denso Wave had a problem: standard barcodes just couldn't hold enough info for the auto parts they were tracking. To fix this, he created the QR (Quick Response) code. Unlike those old-school linear barcodes, QR codes are 2D grids of black and white squares. Think of them as a more advanced language that can be read both horizontally and vertically.
Massive Memory: They use four different encoding modes (numeric, alphanumeric, binary, and Kanji) to pack in way more data than a standard UPC.
Error Correction: Thanks to something called Reed–Solomon correction, a QR code can still work even if it’s a bit scratched or dirty.
Speed: Those three squares in the corners (fiducial markers) help your camera instantly figure out which way is up, making scanning nearly instant. What started as a tool for car factories has turned into a universal digital bridge. Today, we use them for everything from tracking packages and managing documents to opening restaurant menus and following web links. It’s essentially a high-speed shortcut between the physical world and your phone.

What is a Dynamic UPI QR Code?

Unlike a standard sticker, a dynamic UPI QR code is generated specifically for one transaction. It often includes a pre-set amount and transaction ID, so the customer doesn't have to type the amount manually.

What is the difference between Static and Dynamic QR codes?

Static QR codes have fixed information that cannot be changed once printed. Dynamic QR codes use a redirect link, allowing you to change the destination (like a website URL) without reprinting the actual code.

Do I need to enter my PIN to receive money?

No. This is a common scam. You never need to scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to receive money; these actions are only for sending money.
